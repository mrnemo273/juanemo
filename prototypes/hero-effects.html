01 — Mouse-Responsive Axes
JUANEMO

Mouse X controls width axis (condensed ↔ extended). Mouse Y controls weight (light ↔ black). The text reshapes itself to follow your cursor — every position produces a unique typographic state.

02 — Animated Gradient Fill
JUANEMO

Letters filled with a shifting gradient using background-clip: text. The Bittersweet accent color flows through the letterforms like heat. Could be tied to scroll position instead of auto-animating.

03 — Outline → Fill on Hover
JUANEMO
JUANEMO

Text starts as pure outlines — hollow, architectural. On hover, a solid fill wipes in from left. Could trigger on scroll, on load, or per-character. The outlined state alone is a strong visual identity.

04 — Per-Character Interaction

Each letter is independently addressable. Hover collapses it to condensed/light and lifts it up. Could map to mouse proximity (letters near cursor react), staggered load animations, or per-character axis randomization.

05 — Variable Font Entrance
JUANEMO

Text enters as ultra-condensed whispers with wide letter-spacing, then expands to fill the space — weight, width, and optical size all animate together. The text doesn't just appear, it ARRIVES.

Hover to activate. Layered copies with clip-path slicing and color offset create a controlled digital glitch. Channels of Bittersweet bleed through displaced fragments. Aggressive, technical, very Flash-era.

07 — Axis Breathing
JUANEMO

The text slowly oscillates between bold-extended and light-narrow — like breathing. Width, weight, and tracking all shift in a continuous loop. The font is alive. Subtle enough for ambient background behavior.

JUANEMO

A Bittersweet line sweeps across, transforming the text from ultra-light condensed to bold extended as it passes. Two typographic states coexist — the reveal IS the Bittersweet accent rule, reimagined as motion.
